# Business Model

## Customer Segments

Who exactly you serve — specific segments, not generic labels.
Each segment maps to a potential audience landing page.

-

## Value Propositions (per segment)

What you promise each segment — different segments may value
different aspects. Maps to service pages and hero copy.

-

## Channels

How customers find and engage you — informs SEO strategy,
content topics, and where to focus marketing copy.

-

## Customer Relationships

How you build and maintain relationships — informs CTA type,
follow-up content, and post-purchase pages.

-

## Revenue Streams

How money flows — per offer, pricing model, payment structure.
Informs pricing page decisions and quote/booking flows.

-

## Key Activities

What you do to deliver — informs process sections, about page,
and "how we work" content.

-

## Key Resources

What you need to deliver — team, tools, expertise. Informs
about page, trust signals, and technology credibility sections.

-

## Key Partnerships

Who you work with — informs trust signals, logo bars, and
"powered by" mentions.

-

## Cost Structure

Major cost drivers — not shown on site, but informs pricing
rationale and "why we're affordable" positioning.

-

## Competitive Advantages

What's genuinely hard for competitors to copy — the real moat.
This drives differentiation copy across every page.

-

## Metrics That Matter

KPIs the business tracks — informs stats bar, proof sections,
and "results we deliver" content.

-

## Conversion Architecture

Full buyer journey: awareness → consideration → decision → action.
Maps the exact content needed at each stage.
