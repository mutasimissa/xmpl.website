# Content Strategy Framework

## Content levels

1. **Core conversion pages** — Home, Solutions, Contact (directly drive the
   primary CTA)
2. **Proof pages** — Case Studies, Testimonials (build trust through evidence)
3. **Supporting education pages** — FAQ, Industries, Blog (address objections,
   capture informational intent)
4. **Legal and trust pages** — Privacy Policy, Terms (compliance and baseline
   trust)

## Content hierarchy scoring

Score each page on a 1-5 scale for these dimensions:

| Dimension              | 1 (weak)                      | 5 (strong)                                        |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Conversion impact**  | No CTA, informational only    | Directly drives primary conversion                |
| **Trust contribution** | No proof elements             | Strong proof with specific evidence               |
| **SEO value**          | No keyword targeting          | Primary commercial keyword with clear intent      |
| **Buyer journey fit**  | Doesn't map to a buying stage | Clearly serves awareness, evaluation, or decision |

Prioritize pages scoring 4+ across all dimensions for first implementation.

## Content priorities

1. Homepage clarity — visitor understands the business within 5 seconds
2. Offer pages — buyer can evaluate fit without speaking to sales
3. Proof and trust — claims are supported with credible evidence
4. SEO intent alignment — pages match what buyers actually search for
5. Objection handling — common concerns are addressed proactively
6. Contact conversion — conversion path is clear, low-friction, and reassuring

## Writing rules

- Write for decision-makers first — assume they are busy and skeptical
- Keep claims supportable — if you can't prove it, don't say it
- Prefer specific business language over marketing fluff
- Use scannable section structures — short paragraphs, bullet points, clear
  headings
- Lead with outcomes, not features
- Address the buyer's problem before presenting the solution
- Include a clear next step on every page
- Match tone to the brand strategy — every sentence should sound like this brand

## Content quality signals

- Headline communicates a clear benefit
- First paragraph answers "why should I care?"
- Proof appears within the first scroll
- CTA appears at least twice per long page (after value, after proof)
- FAQ addresses real buyer objections, not filler questions

## Blog / content marketing

Blog content serves three purposes: capturing informational search intent,
building topical authority (E-E-A-T), and creating internal linking
opportunities to commercial pages.

### Content cluster model

Each service pillar should have a content cluster:

1. **Pillar page** — the service/solution page (commercial intent)
2. **Cluster posts** — 3-5 blog posts on related informational topics
3. **Internal linking** — every cluster post links to the pillar; the pillar
   links back

### Blog post quality bar

- Minimum 800 words for informational posts, 1200+ for pillar topics
- H1 contains primary keyword naturally
- Author bio with credentials (E-E-A-T)
- At least 2 internal links to service pages
- At least 1 internal link to a related blog post
- FAQ section with 2-3 questions (for schema markup) where relevant
- Meta description with keyword + value proposition

### Publishing cadence

- Quality over quantity — 1-2 well-researched posts per month beats daily filler
- Each post should map to a keyword with real search volume
- Prioritize posts that support commercial pages over standalone topics
